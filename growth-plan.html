THE RULES
Non-Negotiable Scaling Principles

1. Revenue Before Hiring

Never hire based on projected revenue. Only hire when current revenue DEMANDS it. The revenue comes first, the hire follows. Not the other way around.

2. Contractors Before Full-Time

Every role starts as a contractor (1099). If they perform for 3 months, convert to full-time (W-2). This protects you from bad hires.

3. Offshore for Execution, Local for Sales

Developers, VAs, content creators: hire from Philippines, Latin America, Eastern Europe. Save 60-70% on labor. Sales reps: ALWAYS local. In-person closing is your edge.

4. Every Hire Must Generate 3x Their Cost

A $4K/mo developer should enable $12K/mo in revenue. A $2K/mo sales manager should bring in $6K+. If they don't, the hire was wrong.

5. Systemize Before You Scale

If only YOU can do a task, you can't hire for it. Document every process: how to build a site, how to onboard a client, how to run an ad campaign. SOPs first, then delegation.

6. Cash Reserve = 3 Months of Payroll

Before adding any employee, you must have 3 months of their salary in the bank. At 100 employees ($280K/mo payroll), that's $840K cash reserve. Build it gradually.
