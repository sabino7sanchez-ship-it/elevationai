THE BASICS
What Is Wholesale Real Estate?

The Simple Version

You find a homeowner who wants to sell fast (motivated seller). You put their house under contract at a discount. Then you sell that CONTRACT (not the house) to a cash buyer/investor for a fee. You never own the property. You never need a mortgage. You need $0 to start. Your profit = the assignment fee ($5K-$20K per deal).

THE MATH
The Wholesale Formula
Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO)
MAO = ARV × 70% − Repairs − Your Fee
ARV = After Repair Value (what the house is worth fixed up)

Example Deal

  • House ARV (fixed up): $500,000
  • 70% of ARV: $350,000
  • Estimated repairs: $50,000
  • Your assignment fee: $15,000
  • MAO (your max offer to seller): $285,000
  • You contract at $285K → assign to buyer at $300K → you pocket $15,000

THE PROCESS
8 Steps to Your First Deal
1

Form Your LLC

File with California Secretary of State ($70). Name it something like "Sanchez Property Solutions LLC." This protects you legally and makes you look professional. File your BOI report with FinCEN (required 2026 — $500 penalty if you forget).

2

Learn Your Target Markets

Your areas: Pico Rivera, Norwalk, Downey, Bellflower, Long Beach, Torrance, Anaheim. Study comps on Zillow/Redfin. Know what houses sell for in each zip code. You need to spot a deal in 30 seconds.

3

Build Your Cash Buyers List FIRST

Before you find a single deal, build a list of 20+ cash buyers. Search your county recorder for recent cash purchases. Join local REI meetups (REIA). Join BiggerPockets. Post in Facebook groups: "I wholesale off-market properties in [city]. Interested buyers DM me." No buyers = no business.

4

Find Motivated Sellers

Free methods: Driving for Dollars (drive neighborhoods, look for distressed properties — overgrown yards, boarded windows, code violations), Craigslist "for sale by owner," Facebook Marketplace, Zillow FSBOs. Paid methods: PropStream ($99/mo — pull distressed property lists), direct mail campaigns, PPC ads ("sell my house fast [city]"), bandit signs (check local laws), cold calling absentee owners.

5

Talk to the Seller (The Conversation)

Your goal: find their pain point and timeline. Ask: "Why are you selling?" "What's your ideal timeline?" "What do you owe on the house?" "Would you be open to a cash offer that closes in 2 weeks?" Be honest — tell them you're an investor. Build rapport. Solve their problem.

6

Run the Numbers & Make an Offer

Use the calculator above. Pull comps (3-5 similar homes sold in last 6 months within 1 mile). Estimate repairs (use contractor contacts — Santiago can help). Calculate MAO. Make the offer BELOW MAO to leave negotiation room. Always offer in writing.

7

Get It Under Contract

Use a standard Purchase and Sale Agreement with an assignment clause: "Buyer reserves the right to assign this contract to a third party." Include an inspection period (14-21 days) as your escape clause. Put up $500-$1,000 earnest money deposit (refundable during inspection period).

8

Assign the Contract & Get Paid

Send the deal to your cash buyers list. First serious buyer gets it. They sign an Assignment of Contract and pay your assignment fee at closing. The title company handles everything — you get a check at the closing table. Done.

LEGAL
California Wholesale Laws (2026)

What's Legal

  • Assigning contracts for a fee — 100% legal in California
  • No real estate license required (as long as you're assigning YOUR contract, not brokering)
  • Collecting assignment fees at closing
  • Marketing your CONTRACT rights (not the property itself)

What Can Get You in Trouble

Do NOT: Market the property as if you own it. Act as a broker between buyer and seller without a license. Do more than 1-2 deals without an LLC. Fail to disclose that you're an investor assigning the contract. Skip the BOI report for your LLC ($500+ fine).

Required Disclosures

  • Tell the seller: "I am a real estate investor and may assign this contract to another buyer for a profit"
  • Include assignment language in the purchase agreement
  • Disclose your fee if asked (you're not required to volunteer it, but don't lie)
  • Never advertise the property address as "for sale" — you don't own it
SCRIPTS
Seller Conversation Scripts

Cold Call to Property Owner

"Hi, is this [Owner Name]? My name is Sabino with Sanchez Property Solutions. I'm a local real estate investor and I noticed you own a property at [address]. I'm reaching out because I buy houses in the area for cash, and I was curious — have you ever thought about selling? Or is there any chance you'd consider a fair cash offer? [IF YES/MAYBE]: Great. I'd love to learn more about the property. Can I ask you a few quick questions? - What's the current condition of the house? - Are you currently living there or is it a rental? - What's your timeline — are you looking to sell soon or just exploring? - Do you have a price in mind? - Is there a mortgage on it? [Based on answers, run your numbers and make an offer] I can typically close in 14-21 days, all cash, and I cover the closing costs. Would you be open to meeting at the property so I can take a look?"

Driving for Dollars Script (Knocking on Door)

"Hey there! My name's Sabino, I'm a local real estate investor. I was driving through the neighborhood and noticed your property. I buy houses in this area for cash, and I wanted to see if you or the owner might be interested in a fair offer. I know that's kind of out of the blue — but I work with homeowners who want to sell fast without dealing with agents, open houses, or repairs. I buy as-is, close in 2-3 weeks, and cover all closing costs. Is this something you'd be open to chatting about?"

Follow-Up Text After Initial Contact

Hey [Name], this is Sabino from Sanchez Property Solutions. We spoke about your property at [address]. I ran some numbers and I'd like to make you a fair cash offer. Would you have 15 minutes this week to chat? I can come to you. No pressure at all — just want to show you what I can do 🤝

Week 4: Get Under Contract

  • Follow up with every seller from week 3
  • Make 5 offers (expect 1-2 to stick)
  • Get at least 1 property under contract
  • Blast the deal to your buyers list
  • Close and collect your first assignment fee
